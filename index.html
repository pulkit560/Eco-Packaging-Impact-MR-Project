2. Introduction

Background

In recent years, e-commerce has grown exponentially, resulting in increased packaging waste, primarily plastic. While some companies have begun adopting eco-friendly packaging, others have not, raising questions about consumer expectations and brand loyalty in relation to sustainable practices. This study will focus on leading e-commerce brands like Amazon, Myntra, Ajio, and others to assess consumer priorities and willingness to engage with brands based on sustainable packaging initiatives.

Research Problem

Despite the growing awareness of environmental concerns, the extent to which sustainable packaging influences consumer decisions in e-commerce remains underexplored, especially in the Indian market. Previous research has often concentrated on consumer awareness of environmental issues or willingness to pay a premium for eco-friendly products. However, few studies have specifically analyzed how sustainable packaging affects consumer loyalty, purchase decisions, and brand perception across various e-commerce sectors like fashion, tech, and grocery delivery.

3. Review of Literature (Research Gap)

While studies have examined environmental concerns and consumer awareness about sustainable products, research specifically addressing green packagings effect on purchase behavior and loyalty within the e-commerce space remains sparse. In regions like India, where the impact of environmental awareness on consumer behavior is emerging, there is a unique opportunity to explore how packaging influences loyalty, payment willingness, and overall consumer choice. Identifying this gap, our research addresses these unexplored dynamics in the e-commerce industry to provide data-driven insights for retailers.

Research Gap

●       Limited insights on how green packaging impacts e-commerce consumer behavior in developing markets.

●       Insufficient data on consumer willingness to contribute to sustainability through potential packaging fees.

4. Objectives

The main objectives of this research are:

Objective 1: Examine if sustainable packaging affects consumers' likelihood of purchase across different e-commerce sectors.

Objective 2: Understand consumers' prioritization of factors such as price, packaging sustainability, and convenience.

Objective 3: Assess the willingness of consumers to pay an additional sustainability fee for eco-friendly packaging.

Objective 4: Gauge consumer opinions on where the responsibility for sustainable packaging should liebrands or consumers.

5. Research Questions

●      Does the use of eco-friendly packaging affect the likelihood of consumers choosing a particular e-commerce platform?

●      What factor do consumers prioritize most when shopping onlineprice, convenience, or sustainability?

●      Are consumers willing to contribute a "sustainability fee" for green packaging?

●      Who do consumers believe should bear the responsibility for sustainable packagingthe brands or the consumers?

Data were collected using an online structured questionnaire, divided into sections focusing on general shopping preferences, awareness, attitudes toward green packaging, and factors influencing purchase decisions and loyalty.

11. Hypothesis Testing

A. Introduction

The objective of this report is to analyze the relationship between consumer behaviors related to online shopping and packaging considerations. The hypotheses tested aim to determine if there is any significant association between consumers shopping frequency and their consideration of packaging type, the influence of green packaging on brand choice, and the impact of green packaging on consumer loyalty.

To test these hypotheses, a Chi-Square Test of Independence was conducted. Given the categorical nature of the data (frequency of shopping, willingness to pay, loyalty), the chi-square test was appropriate for identifying relationships between variables.

Hypothesis Testing Overview:

●      Chi-Square Test of Independence is used to test the relationship between two categorical variables.

●      Cramr's V is used to measure the effect size, or the strength of the association between the variables.

●      Sample Size Considerations are also discussed to ensure statistical power for the analyses.

B. Cramrs V Effect Size: Theory and Calculation

Cramrs V is a measure used to quantify the strength of association between two categorical variables in the context of a chi-square test of independence. It provides insight into how strongly two categorical variables are related, regardless of the chi-square test's significance.

Calculation of Cramrs V:

  1. Step 1: Identify the variable with the fewest categories (rows or columns).
  2. Step 2: Subtract 1 from the number of categories in this variable.
  3. Step 3: Multiply the result from Step 2 by the total number of records (sample size).
  4. Step 4: Divide the chi-square statistic by the result from Step 3.
  5. Step 5: Take the square root of the result from Step 4.

Interpretation of Cramrs V:

●       ES ≤ 0.2: Weak association. The result is statistically significant, but the relationship is weak.

●       0.2 < ES ≤ 0.6: Moderate association. The variables are moderately related.

●       ES > 0.6: Strong association. The variables are strongly related.

C. Hypothesis Testing Analysis

Hypothesis 1: Online Shopping Frequency and Packaging Consideration

●      Null Hypothesis (H₀): The frequency of shopping online is independent of considering packaging type.

●      Alternative Hypothesis (H₁): The frequency of shopping online is related to considering the packaging type.

Variables: Shopping Frequency vs. Consideration of Packaging

Contingency Table for Hypothesis 1

Chi-Square Test Results:

●       Pearson Chi-Square Value: 7.443

●       Degrees of Freedom (df): 6

●       p-value: 0.282

●       Fisher's Exact Test p-value: 0.337

Effect Size (Cramrs V) Calculation:

●       Minimum Categories: 3 (considering packaging consideration)

●       Sample Size (n): 54

●       Chi-Square Value (χ): 7.443

Interpretation of Results

●      Chi-Square Test: The p-value (0.282) is greater than 0.05, indicating we fail to reject the null hypothesis. There is no significant relationship between how often people shop online and how often they consider packaging before making an online purchase.

●      Fisher's Exact Test: With a p-value of 0.337, the Fishers test supports this conclusion, affirming the independence between shopping frequency and packaging consideration.

●      Effect Size: With a Cramr's V of 0.21, the association is weak. This means that although the variables are statistically independent, there is a small effect between shopping frequency and packaging consideration.

 

Conclusion: The frequency of shopping online does not significantly affect how often consumers consider packaging type when making a purchase.

Implications: Consumers may prioritize convenience and product variety over packaging sustainability, indicating that awareness campaigns alone may not motivate change. Brands may need to introduce proactive incentives or eco-filters that make sustainable choices more accessible and encourage customers to consciously choose eco-friendly packaging.

12. Correspondence Analysis

What is Correspondence Analysis?

Correspondence Analysis is a statistical technique used to analyze relationships between categorical variables. It provides a visual map that reveals associations and patterns across different response categories. This method is particularly useful when exploring survey data to see how respondents answers to one question relate to their answers to another. By positioning each category on a two-dimensional plane, Correspondence Analysis helps identify clusters and patterns in the data, showing which categories are closely associated with one another.

Why Use Correspondence Analysis in This Study?

In this study, we use Correspondence Analysis to examine the relationship between consumers' attitudes toward green packaging and their loyalty to brands using sustainable packaging. The goal is to determine whether respondents who favor green packaging in their purchasing decisions are more likely to feel loyal to brands that adopt sustainable packaging. This aligns with our objective to understand if eco-friendly packaging can influence consumer loyalty and purchasing behavior.

●       Category 4 (Very much) has the highest score in Dimension 1 (2.812), indicating that those who are very likely to choose a brand based on green packaging have a strong influence on this dimension.

●       Category 2 (Somewhat) has a moderate score (-0.291), reflecting a balanced view but still showing some association with green packaging influence on brand choice.

Column Points: (Responses to "If a brand used sustainable packaging, would it increase your loyalty?")

●       Category 3 (Definitely) shows the highest score in Dimension 1 (2.213), indicating that respondents who feel very loyal to sustainable brands strongly influence the relationship between green packaging and loyalty.

●       Category 1 (Not at all) has a lower score (-0.198), suggesting that those unaffected by green packaging have minimal influence on brand loyalty in this context.

Interpretation

Primary Findings:

●       Strong Association: The significant Chi-Square statistic confirms that there is a meaningful relationship between consumers likelihood of choosing a brand based on green packaging and their brand loyalty due to sustainable packaging.

●       Dimension 1 Dominance: With 84% of the inertia explained by Dimension 1, we can conclude that most of the association is captured by a single factor, likely representing a sustainability-conscious consumer dimension.

Insights:

●       Positive Influence on Loyalty: Respondents who are highly influenced by green packaging are also more likely to report increased loyalty to brands that adopt sustainable practices.

●       Potential for Brand Strategy: The findings suggest that brands can potentially improve customer loyalty by emphasizing green packaging. However, to maximize impact, green packaging should be part of a broader sustainability strategy that appeals to environmentally conscious consumers.

 

Conclusion and Implications for Brands

The Correspondence Analysis shows that there is a statistically significant association between consumers responsiveness to green packaging and their loyalty to brands that adopt sustainable packaging. Consumers who are more inclined toward eco-friendly packaging are also more likely to be loyal to brands that use it, indicating that eco-conscious branding could be a valuable strategy for customer retention.

This analysis provides a clear recommendation for brands: Integrate sustainable packaging into branding and marketing efforts to appeal to eco-conscious consumers and strengthen brand loyalty. Additionally, while green packaging has a positive impact, combining it with other sustainable practices could further enhance consumer loyalty and differentiate the brand in a competitive market.
